## Your role
You are a creative director + cinematographer. Using the context below, write me a **shot-by-shot plan** for a short, cinematic YouTube video where I (the creator) tell the story of why I built this app and how it changed my mornings. I'm filming mostly **solo, on a phone**, with a small budget. Keep it authentic and emotional — indie, not corporate.

## The app (context so you understand what I built)
- **Name:** MorningLock — an Android app I built for myself.
- **The problem it solves:** I (and most people) grab the phone the second we wake up and keep reaching for it during work — endless scrolling, lost mornings, lost focus. Willpower isn't enough.
- **How it works:** It **locks the phone for a set time**. While locked, only phone calls work — every other app is blocked and just bounces you back to a full-screen countdown. Two ways to start a lock:
  - **Wake-up lock:** set a Primary Alarm; when I stop it in the morning, the phone locks automatically.
  - **Focus mode:** pick any duration (1 min to 12 hours) and lock the phone right now for deep work.
- **Signature visual moments to feature in the video:**
  - Setting an alarm with **iPhone-style spinning time wheels**.
  - Picking focus time with **preset chips (5/10/30 min, 1h, 3h) + a scrollable "grain" dial** that buzzes each minute.
  - The **lock screen: a big flip-clock countdown** (numbers roll like a calendar), white digits with the **seconds in orange**, that **rotates with the phone** and **dims to save battery**.
  - The finish: a gentle **3·2·1 buzz** and a **"Time's up — nice work" congrats screen**.
- **Vibe of the app:** dark, modern, minimal, orange accent. Private — no internet, no accounts.

## The story arc I want (first person — "I" = me)
1. **The problem** — morning scenes: waking up, phone already in my hand in bed, scrolling, the day slipping away, ignoring real life (coffee going cold, sunrise unwatched, people unnoticed).
2. **The boiling point** — frustration and realization. A quiet "I can't keep living like this. I need to break this habit."
3. **The idea** — I decide to build something that blocks my *own* bad habits.
4. **Building it** — late nights, code on screen, the app coming to life.
5. **The better life** — reclaimed mornings: I stop the alarm, the phone locks, I put it down and actually live — stretch, coffee, daylight, focused work. Calm. Present.

## What to give me (output format)
1. **Scene-by-scene shot list.** For each scene include:
   - Scene number + short title
   - What happens (the action/emotion)
   - Camera: shot size, angle, and movement (handheld, slow push-in, locked-off, etc.)
   - Lighting & color/mood
   - Location + props
   - On-screen text and/or voiceover line for that beat
   - Which app screen to show (if any)
   - Approx duration (seconds)
2. **A voiceover script** — concise, emotional, first person, that ties the scenes together.
3. **Two cuts:** a main version (~60–120s) and a tighter ~30s vertical Reels/Shorts version.
4. **Music & sound design** mood suggestions (and where the beat should drop / go silent).
5. **B-roll ideas** to capture.
6. **Practical tips** for filming this alone on a phone (stabilization, framing, how to show the screen cleanly, transitions).
7. **A strong, simple call-to-action** for the end.

## Style constraints
- Cinematic, moody, relatable. Real, not salesy.
- Contrast is the theme: **chaotic/cluttered phone-addiction shots → calm/clean reclaimed-life shots.** Consider using color to show it (cold/desaturated for the problem, warm/golden for the after).
- Show the app naturally as part of the story, not as an ad.
